We want to explore our precious territory and its ancient villages, adapting to the environment that surrounds us, preserving our land and its fruits, safeguarding the noble trades of the farmer and the breeder who live and work there.

The project

Woodvivors is a project born in 2016 from an idea by Francesco Paolo Lanzino. Our goal is to highlight a forgotten reality of rural Italy, collecting the testimonies of the protagonists of this world. The team is formed by a group of people eager to rediscover and communicate the traditions, values ​​and lifestyles of the past;

With our journey we want to show how anyone, armed with curiosity, can bring to light the rural roots of their own territory – even when they seem very far away - to create a sustainable future that does not deny our past.

Ambitions

The realization of a documentary film, supported by an online magazine, aimed at highlighting a forgotten part of our country;

Valorization of the Italian natural heritage, in particular of the hill towns, of the Natural Parks and of the hiking trails;

GPS mapping of the entire route and every water source we encounter along the way, in order to create a virtual map of wells, sources and drinking water, available online in open source;

Recovery of the residual testimonies in order to allow a compatible reuse of the ancient wisdoms linked to the territory destined to be lost forever.

The route

The itinerary winds through Italy, from Palermo to Turin. The route develops mainly along Apennine ridges, far from the large urban centers, following the traces of the old paths and sheep tracks where possible. The expected duration of the trip is about 5 months, including the amount of the time required for realizing the audiovisual production;

The journey crosses some of the most interesting natural and historical sites of our country: Pollino National Park, Cinque Terre, The Apennines, Peloritani, and these are just some of the wonderful places that will touch the project. It is necessary to travel during spring and summer, taking advantage of the warmer months, with more hours of light and the possibility to find fresh pastures for the sustenance of our animals.
